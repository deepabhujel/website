Types

Substance Abuse Rehabilitation:
Focuses on treating drug or alcohol dependency through detoxification, therapy, and long-term support to prevent relapse.
Physical Rehabilitation:
Aims to restore physical function and mobility for those recovering from injuries, surgeries, or disabilities.
Mental Health Rehabilitation:
Provides support for individuals managing conditions such as depression, anxiety, or schizophrenia, often through counseling and psychiatric care.

Therapeutic Services:

Medical Supervision:
Many centers offer 24/7 medical care, including support for those with physical or withdrawal symptoms.
Counseling and Therapy:
Individual and group therapy sessions help individuals understand and address underlying issues.
Occupational and Physical Therapy:
For physical rehabilitation, these therapies help clients regain independence .

Staff and Environment:

Staff in a rehabilitation center usually include healthcare professionals, such as doctors, nurses, therapists, counselors, and social workers, all trained to support the recovery process.
The environment is often structured to provide stability, routine, and comfort, offering a calm, drug-free, and supportive atmosphere that encourages healing.
